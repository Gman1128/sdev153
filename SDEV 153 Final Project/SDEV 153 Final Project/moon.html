The Moon

What is The Moon?

The moon is a large and round object that orbits our planet Earth and helps reflect the light off the sun. It is a barren planetoid with no life where the temperature is always cold. It is essential for Earth and us because the gravational pull from it helps stablize Earth's axil tilt, which stabilixes the climates cycles across the globe. It is also needed to create tides for us becuase they have played a significant role for the development of life for our plaent by providing a predictible rhythm for our coastal ecosystems. Without the moon, the climate would be more extreme and life wouldn't exist.

How Was The Moon Formed?

The moon was fromed around 4.5 billion years of go, shortly after the Earth was formed. It started forming when an large celestial body, which is often referred to as "Theia", roughly around the size of the planet Mars collided with the Earth when it was still young. During the collison between the two objects, debris flew out into orbit. The debris would eventually clump together due to gravity over a period of time. The Moon was eventually created and now orbits around Earth.
