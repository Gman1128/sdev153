The Sun

What is The Sun?

The Sun is a star at the very center of our solar system. It is a giant, massive sphere of plasma. It is the primary source for energy for life on Earth. The gravity of the sun helps keep the solar system together, making sure that planets, asteroids, comets, or any space debris stay in their respective orbits. It generaes light and heat and helps support life on our planet.

How Was The Sun Formed?

The Sun fromed around 4.6 billion years of go, during the time before our solar sytem was even formed. It started forming in a massive spinning cloud of gas and dust called a solar nebula. When the nebula started to collapse under the weight of its own gravity, it spun faster and faster and started to flatten into a disk. Most materials drifting through space started to be pulled toward the center and begun forming our Sun. Any material left started drifting together in big clump form the planets of our solar system.
